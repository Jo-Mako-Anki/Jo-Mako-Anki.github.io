What is Anki

There are two simple concepts behind Anki: active recall testing and spaced repetition.

Active Recall Testing

Active recall testing means being asked a question and trying to remember the answer. This is in contrast to passive study, where we read, watch or listen to something without pausing to consider if we know the answer

How Anki works

Fields

A field is where you put in your information. By default you have two fields called "Front" and "Back". In my example above I created a lot more fields for each specific piece of information: Japanese sentence, English sentence, screenshot, etc.


Creating many fields comes in handy when editing and styling the card. Let's say I have my vocabulary and my sentence in the same field called "Front". If I wanted to increase the size of the text, I'll have to increase both at the same time. Having 2 separate fields, I can have different sizes for the word and the sentence.

Graduating Interval

Using your results to refine the settings

80 to 90%

Reviews are more effective when they are difficult. The harder the review, the more it boosts your memory. But if you can't remember the answer at all, then it's too hard. So you should aim for a result between 80 to 90%.
